Privacy Policy
Effective Date: April 30, 2026  |  Contact: support@ardentime.com

Arden ("Company," "we," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights with respect to that information. By installing or using the Arden application ("Software"), you agree to the practices described below.

1. What Arden Does Not Collect
Arden is built on a local-first architecture. Customer Content is stored on your device and, when you are signed in, synced to Company's cloud database (hosted on Supabase) scoped to your authenticated account. The following data is never transmitted to any third party for storage beyond Supabase:
The content of your documents, files, or screen activity

When you are signed in, the following data is synced to Supabase so that it is accessible from the Arden web application and across your devices:
Your client names, matter names, and profile information
Time entry narratives and classifications generated by the Software
Cloud-synced data is protected by row-level security; only your authenticated account can access it.

Note: Activity metadata (app names, window titles, timestamps, and durations) along with your client and matter information are transmitted to our classification API for processing, as described in Section 4. This data is used solely to classify your time entries and is not stored by any third party.

If you use Arden's Clio integration, time entries you choose to send to Clio are transmitted to and stored by Clio in accordance with their own privacy policy. This transmission occurs at your explicit direction and is governed by your agreement with Clio.

2. Information We Do Collect
2.1 Account and Authentication Data
When you create an Arden account, we collect:
Your email address and hashed password (via Supabase Auth), or
Your Google account identifier if you choose Google OAuth sign-in
Your subscription and billing status

This data is stored in Arden's Supabase-hosted backend and is used solely to authenticate you, manage your subscription, and provide account support.

2.2 Usage Telemetry
Arden automatically collects limited usage telemetry to monitor application health and improve the product. This telemetry data includes:
App open and close events
Session uptime (in minutes)
Number of activity events collected per session
Number of chunks classified per session
A device-level UUID (randomly generated, not tied to your identity) and a user_id linked to your account

Telemetry is first stored locally in ardentrack.db on your device and is synced to Arden's servers (hosted on Supabase) approximately every 24 hours and upon application close.

We do not use telemetry data to train large language models (LLMs) or generative AI features.

3. How We Use Your Information
Account data is used to authenticate you, manage your license, and provide customer support.
Telemetry data is used to monitor application stability, measure feature engagement, and prioritize product improvements.
We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties for marketing purposes.

4. Third-Party Service Providers
Arden relies on the following third-party infrastructure providers to operate. Each receives only the minimum data necessary for their function:

4.1 Supabase
Supabase (supabase.com) hosts Arden's authentication backend, telemetry database, and cloud-synced Customer Content (time entries, matters, clients, and profile data). Supabase receives your account credentials (email/hashed password or OAuth token), anonymized telemetry records, and the Customer Content you generate while signed in. All cloud-stored data is scoped to your authenticated account using row-level security. Supabase's privacy practices are governed by their Privacy Policy at supabase.com/privacy.

4.2 Render
Render (render.com) hosts the classification API that processes your activity data. During classification, a 6-minute chunk of activity metadata (app names, window titles, timestamps, and durations) along with your client and matter information are transmitted to Render's servers, combined with our internal system prompt, and forwarded to OpenAI. Arden does not connect external log drains in Render, and our application is configured not to log request or response content. All data in transit is encrypted via TLS. Render's privacy practices are available at render.com/privacy.

4.3 OpenAI
OpenAI (openai.com) provides the AI model (currently GPT-4o mini 5.0) used to classify your activity chunks. To perform classification, OpenAI receives activity metadata (app names, window titles, timestamps, and durations) along with your client and matter information. This is the minimum information required to accurately assign time entries to the correct matter. OpenAI does not receive the content of your documents or files. Arden has disabled two data-sharing settings in the OpenAI organization dashboard: "Share inputs and outputs with OpenAI" and "API call logging" are both set to Disabled. This ensures that no data submitted via the API is stored by OpenAI or used to train its models. OpenAI's privacy practices are available at openai.com/policies/privacy-policy.

4.4 ArdenTrack
ArdenTrack is Arden's proprietary activity-tracking system, bundled with the Software. It runs as a local process on your device and does not transmit data externally. ArdenTrack collects only app names, window titles, timestamps, and durations - never the content of your work. This data is stored locally and used solely as the input to Arden's classification pipeline.
